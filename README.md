GOTTFRIED LEIBNIZ (1646–1716) — LOGIKĒ VI, the HINGE between Llull's wheel and Boole's algebra. Llull combined ideas; Leibniz dreamed the whole machine: a UNIVERSAL SYMBOLIC LANGUAGE (characteristica universalis) + a CALCULUS OF REASONING (calculus ratiocinator), so two people who disagree would not argue but compute — 'CALCULEMUS!', settle it like a sum. ⚑ LIVE: (1) BINARY — toggle 8 bits → any number as a pattern of 0/1 (the register, the only thing a gate can read; he formalised base-2 in 1703, 250 yrs before the machines); (2) 'CALCULEMUS' MADE LITERAL — his actual 1679 idea: give each attribute a PRIME, let a concept be the PRODUCT of its attributes' primes, and a syllogism becomes a DIVISION (man = animal×rational = 2×3 = 6; 'all men are animal?' ⟺ 2 divides 6 = VALID; build the concept, watch inferences settle by arithmetic — verified). He built the four-operation Stepped Reckoner; our calculus notation dx, ∫ is his (despite the Newton priority war). CONFIDENCE-TIERED: STRONG (binary, the engine, and 'Calculemus!' the vision all computing pursues) / UNFINISHED (the characteristica & calculus were a lifelong PROGRAM never completed; his logical algebra sat UNPUBLISHED ~200 yrs, so Boole re-found it independently) / IMPOSSIBLE-IN-FULL (⚑ the deepest twist — Gödel 1931 & Turing 1936 proved NO method decides EVERY truth by calculation; reasoning CAN be mechanised — that half runs on your desk — but not completely; Leibniz got the direction exactly right and the limit exactly wrong).
